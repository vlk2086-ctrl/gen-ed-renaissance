Process & Timeline

Where we are, and what this means for you

We plan to implement changes to the general education requirements of all undergraduate programs beginning Spring 2028, continuing through Fall 2028. This is a monumental moment in SCAD's curriculum history — it will affect every undergraduate student at SCAD, and that's exactly why we want engagement from all corners of the university throughout the process.

Now

Research phase

We are still in the research phase of the project, initiated and facilitated by the Office of Curriculum and Assessment. We're gathering both primary data — surveys, interviews, focus groups — and secondary data, so that any future changes are grounded in evidence rather than assumption.

Next

Broadening participation

As the research continues, we'll be asking for further participation from faculty, and eventually students. Watch this site — especially the Discussion channels — for opportunities to weigh in.

Fall 2027 – Spring 2028

Building

We'll be building new courses and content based on all of the data collected from stakeholders during the research phase — which is exactly why participation now matters so much. What we hear from you directly shapes what gets built.

Spring 2028 – Fall 2028

Implementation

Following the research and building phases, updated general education requirements roll out for all undergraduate programs, beginning Spring 2028 and continuing through Fall 2028.

How you may be asked to get involved: data gathering, skills affinity groups, course creation, syllabi planning, and assignment building are all possible ways faculty may contribute as this moves forward. We're genuinely excited to engage with you throughout this process — reach out anytime at curriculum@scad.edu.
Research Insights

What the study is built on

Multi-stakeholder survey design

Employers, alumni, academic leaders, and faculty are surveyed in phases — starting with academic leaders — using a shared competency-priorities module so responses can be compared directly across groups.

An optional AI Anxiety module

Built on the validated Wang & Wang (2019) AI Anxiety Scale, this optional module lets the study measure AI-related fear alongside openness to curriculum change — without forcing every respondent through it.

Six converging competencies

Critical Thinking, Adaptability & Flexibility, Collaboration & Teamwork, Verbal Communication, Curiosity, and Leadership & Professionalism — selected because they held up both as independently "important" ratings and as forced top-5 choices across employer, alumni, academic leader, and faculty groups.

National benchmarking

Findings are triangulated against established datasets — RNL Student Satisfaction Inventory, NSSE, the Ithaka S+R Faculty Survey, and QS global employer data — so SCAD's results can be read in context, not in isolation.
